# Eyelink — Design Context

> Living design brief. Update this file whenever the brand direction, audience, or aesthetic changes.

## Users

- **Primary**: People with hearing, visual, and mobility impairments in India (ages 18–65) who depend on daily support for communication, navigation, and transport.
- **Secondary**: Caregivers, family members, and educators who book or coordinate services on the user's behalf.
- **Tertiary**: Disability NGOs, special-education schools, accessibility researchers, and government programs evaluating partnership.
- **Context of use**:
  - **Phone-first** — most target users are on mid-range Android, often on patchy 4G.
  - **High-stakes moments** — booking a cab, calling for help, reading a sign. Anxiety is the baseline.
  - **Low-vision realities** — small text, low-contrast UI, and rapid motion are actively harmful.
  - **Mixed digital literacy** — power users and first-time smartphone owners both.
  - **Linguistic diversity** — English UI today, but 11+ Indian regional languages are on the roadmap.

## Brand Personality

- **Voice**: Calm. Capable. Quietly radical.
- **Tone**: Confident but never patronizing. Medical-grade precision, not startup exuberance. Adult-to-adult.
- **Three-word summary**: *calm · capable · quietly radical*
- **Emotional goals**:
  - **Trust** — "this works when I need it."
  - **Relief** — "I don't have to juggle five apps for one task."
  - **Dignity** — "this treats me like a person, not a case study."

## Aesthetic Direction

- **Theme**: Dark mode is the hero (calm, premium, low sensory load, good for low-vision users who prefer dark). Light mode is a first-class peer — not an afterthought.
- **Visual tone**: Industrial-minimal with editorial warmth. Closer to a well-designed hospital interface than a consumer app. Nothing.tech restraint + Stripe Press typography + the humanity of Braille Institute.
- **References to study**: Nothing.tech (industrial restraint, product-led), Linear (motion discipline), Stripe Press / Sessions (editorial typography, calm), Tobii Dynavox (real AAC company — credibility without being boring).
- **Anti-references** (must NOT look like):
  - Generic AI startup cyan-on-dark with glowing blobs
  - Apple accessibility marketing (too corporate, too American, too committee)
  - Be My Eyes / charity aesthetic (patronizing, dated, well-intentioned pity)
  - Bootstrap admin dashboard (the current state — what we are escaping)
  - Decorative "AI" sparkles, gradient text, side-stripe borders

## Design Principles

1. **Show, don't describe.** The product is emotional. A working ASL demo, a real-time audio nav preview, an actual cab booking flow beats ten paragraphs of marketing copy.
2. **Calm at every interaction.** No urgency theater, no countdown timers, no "limited time" copy. Accessibility users are often anxious. The interface should be a steady hand.
3. **One platform, one experience.** All four products must feel like facets of one thing — same visual language, same motion vocabulary, same tone. Not four products stapled together.
4. **The type does the work.** The mission is in the type itself: **Atkinson Hyperlegible** (Braille Institute) for body, **Schibsted Grotesk** (newspaper editorial) for display. Free, distinctive, and on-brand.
5. **Motion is purposeful.** Every animation answers "why does this move?" with feedback, spatial continuity, or state indication — per Emil Kowalski's framework. No decorative motion, no bouncing logos, no continuous aurora blobs.
6. **Accessibility is the deliverable, not a checklist.** WCAG 2.2 AA minimum. `prefers-reduced-motion` respected. Keyboard navigation visible. Focus rings intact. The irony of an accessibility product with a bad accessibility score is the brand-killer.

## Product Lines (4 pillars, equal weight)

| Pillar | Job to be done | Category color |
|---|---|---|
| **ASL Translator** | Real-time sign-language ↔ speech/text | Warm coral (human, communication) |
| **Audio Navigation** | Scene description, obstacle alerts, voice-guided wayfinding | Cool teal (technical, environmental) |
| **Book Assistant** | Accessible cab booking, on-demand travel assistants | Amber (movement, road) |
| **Wheelchair Routes** | Wheelchair-friendly route discovery, accessibility maps | Soft violet (mobility, dignity) |

## Design System Snapshot

- **Typography**: Schibsted Grotesk (display, 400–900) · Atkinson Hyperlegible (body) · JetBrains Mono (code/specs)
- **Color** (OKLCH): inky navy base · warm cream foreground · terracotta signal accent · 4 category tints above
- **Motion**: ease-out-expo for entrances · 200ms default · 60–80ms stagger · 160ms button press · `prefers-reduced-motion` honored
- **Spacing**: 4pt scale (4, 8, 12, 16, 24, 32, 48, 64, 96) with semantic tokens
- **Radii**: 8px default, 12px cards, 16px hero surfaces, full round for avatars/buttons
- **Type scale**: 1.25 modular ratio, fluid `clamp()` on marketing, fixed rem on product UI

## Out of Scope (For Now)

- Hardware device (AudioNav wearable) — announced but not shipping. The site is honest about this.
- Multilingual UI — English only at launch.
- Light mode parity — light mode is functional but the dark mode is the polished primary.
